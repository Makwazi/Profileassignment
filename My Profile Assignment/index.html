Project Medical Booth

Project idea – Creating the ‘Medical Booth’ The medical booth is a piece of technology meant for the future. What it does is the patient who currently has no diagnosis enters the Medical Booth and they will then get an intensive full body scan which the medical booth will find any complications errors and it will then be able to make a diagnosis and also find solutions or cures based on this diagnosis. The Medical booth will be revolutionary as its technology will be ahead of its years in terms of advancements. It will be able to scan the body then project a hologram which can be viewed from the skin to the bony (including the other layers e.g., muscle). It will also give recommendations based on blood tests and the patients currently standard of life and diet. The medical booth will serve as a resource to places doctors cannot access as well as help billions of people improve their living standards and allow them to lead a long and healthy life. The medical booths technological advancements will also be able to formulate new remedies and exercises required for certain people and it will be able to be used to share data across the world of any problems the world is currently facing. More that 94% of the world’s population are lacking access to essential health services, that’s about 7.2 billion people. With the medical booth it will be able to be placed in multiple locations and it can cater thousands per day. Another reason it needs to be implemented is 1 out of 20 adults are misdiagnosed which could result in serious complications and even death.



The medical booth will have its own built-in interface which will allow people to verbally communicate with is (like Siri). This will allow people to be able to fully communicate with the machinery in terms of questions which they need answered and changes they may need to make. With the Holographic body display this will also allow surgeons and doctors to be fully able to pinpoint the patients most damaged areas and the solutions provided will also be able to help doctors improve their skills and knowledge. Despite the advancements in technology humans have made, in the medical field diagnosis is based on doctor’s knowledge which is why they may be cases of people being misdiagnosed. With this technology its accuracy will almost be 100% (nothing is perfect). With things such as infrared scans, x-ray scans, xt scans, ultrasound, spect and mri scans this will allow medical professionals to also further study the human body and develop an understanding on how our body works, this also means they will be different layers to what we will be able to see on our patients. The medical booth is a chamber like cylinder which a patient steps into, 1 at a time, the user will then select options from a screen which is a tablet they can either use their voice or finger, the information provided will then be registered into the system then all electronics devices removed, and the scan will begin, the whole process will take up to 10 minutes and the results will take 5 minutes to fully process. Other features will also include language translation which is essential for different countries, it will be safe and have regulated radiation emissions which are safe for humans and it will also be theft proof and will have emergency services on dial when the time for it is required.



The equipment required for this project are metals, wires screws bolts to be able to build the whole booth, the software required is one that will need to be coded, first it would be the simple open and close door functions and turn on and off lights then it will intensify to scan for x number of seconds and register and store information. This is the complex side of the software which would need to be built by a software engineer. Other required equipment are embedded scans into one booth and this like scales, pressure plates, and special glass windows will also be required to ensure privacy and safety for patients.


The software required for this project will need many experienced software engineers, they will be able to code the required steps and also check for any mistakes included and add on improvements to the revolutionary project. With the equipment side of things engineers and other specialists will be required to put all the parts together then start wiring it. Finding the required skills wont take too long as they are many people who are willing to work, the hardest part is finding the specialists and then being able to pay them however, If business minded people liked the idea they will be able to donate and make it an even larger project. If the project is successful this would mean that billions of people would be able to access one type of an essential health care service and also it would mean that the level of health and wellbeing will also improve. This will not take jobs from doctors as it will be placed in places which have zero to no healthcare services. This can also mean communities will be able to look after each other when a plague hits and care for the ones in their communities who have been diagnosed.
